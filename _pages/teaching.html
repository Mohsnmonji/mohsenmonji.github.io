--- layout: archive title: "Teaching" permalink: /teaching/ author_profile: true --- # Teaching As a university lecturer, I teach quantitative research methods and applied statistics. In my courses, I integrate experiential learning to connect students with real-world applications of statistical knowledge. My goal is to provide students with the skills they need to understand, interpret, and analyze social data and use statistical knowledge to make a difference and contribute to a more functional and just society. --- ## Teaching Experience ### Lecturer - **Social Statistics**, Department of Sociology and Anthropology, Concordia University (Winter 2025) ### Teaching Assistant - **Statistics II**, Department of Sociology and Anthropology, Concordia University (Winter 2024, Fall 2023 - 3 classes) - **Statistics I**, Department of Sociology and Anthropology, Concordia University (Fall 2025) - **Research Methods**, Department of Sociology and Anthropology, Concordia University (Winter 2022) - **Quantitative Research Methods for Practitioners**, Department of Applied Human Science, Concordia University (Winter 2022) - **Classical Social Theory**, Department of Sociology and Anthropology, Concordia University (Winter 2025, Fall 2024, Winter 2023, Fall 2022, Winter 2022, Fall 2021) ### Tutor - **Social Statistics and Quantitative Research Methods**, Concordia University (2016-2020) Taught quantitative research methods and social statistics to undergraduate students.
